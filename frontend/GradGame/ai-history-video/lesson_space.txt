Our Solar System

Earth is one of eight planets that orbit the Sun. The Sun is a star at the center of our solar system. It gives us light and heat.

The four planets closest to the Sun are called the inner planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. They are rocky worlds. Earth is special because it has liquid water and air we can breathe.

Beyond Mars lies the asteroid belt — many small rocky objects. Then come the giant outer planets: Jupiter and Saturn are mostly gas giants with thick atmospheres. Uranus and Neptune are ice giants, very cold and far from the Sun.

The Moon is Earth’s natural satellite. It orbits Earth and affects our tides. Humans first walked on the Moon in 1969 during the Apollo missions.

Scientists use telescopes on Earth and in space (like the James Webb Space Telescope) to study distant stars, galaxies, and planets around other stars — called exoplanets.

Space exploration helps us learn where we came from and whether life might exist elsewhere in the universe.
